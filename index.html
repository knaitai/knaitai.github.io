KnaiTai

Knowledge-native AI.
Open, by design.

Making knowledge programmable for AI.

Not merely retrieving knowledge around a model—but selecting it, activating it and giving it explicit computational effect.

Maritime behavior understanding

From predicting where a vessel will go to understanding why.

A vessel’s recent track suggests several destinations. The conclusion changes as relevant geographic, vessel, port and historical knowledge becomes active.

Knowledge in action

The prediction begins with observation.
The understanding emerges from knowledge.

In this synthetic example, an observation-only model initially favors Tanjung Pelepas. KnaiTai then activates the knowledge relevant to the situation: the shipping corridor, the vessel’s operating requirements, candidate-port capabilities, geographic constraints and the vessel’s prior calls. Each item has an explicit and inspectable effect on the conclusion.

Geographic knowledge

Where can the vessel plausibly move?

Shipping corridors, coastal geometry and protected waters shape feasible routes and constrain candidate destinations.

Vessel knowledge

What does this vessel require?

Vessel class, draft and berth requirements determine which nearby destinations are operationally suitable.

Port knowledge

Which ports can actually receive it?

Port depth, berth capability and operating characteristics distinguish proximity from true viability.

Historical knowledge

What has this vessel done before?

Prior calls and recurring behavior supply evidence that the current movement trace alone cannot reveal.

The point of Knowledge-native AI: knowledge is not background material surrounding the model. It is selected, made active, given computational effect and kept visible in the resulting reasoning.
